2. What’s Included

Every move-out cleaning includes the following, room by room:

Kitchen

  • Inside and outside refrigerator (must be emptied beforehand)
  • Oven and stovetop deep clean
  • Inside microwave
  • Dishwasher exterior; interior wipe-down
  • Cabinet fronts and insides
  • Sink, faucet, countertops, and backsplash sanitizing
  • Range hood exterior
  • Light switches and outlets

Bathrooms

  • Toilet deep cleaning (inside and out)
  • Shower/tub scrubbing, tile and grout
  • Mirrors and glass fixtures
  • Sink, vanity, and cabinet interior/exterior
  • Floor sanitization
  • Light fixtures and exhaust fan

Throughout the Home

  • Baseboards and trim: dust and wipe-down. Surface dirt removed. Scuffs, scrapes, or marks requiring scrubbing are not included unless called out in the job request.
  • Door frames: dust and wipe-down (same standard as baseboards).
  • Ceiling fans (within reach of standard step ladder or using extension pole)
  • Window sills and tracks (interior)
  • Light fixtures and switches
  • Closet shelves and rods
  • Vacuum all carpets and rugs
  • Mop all hard floors
  • General dusting of all surfaces
  • Wall spot-cleaning (light marks only): included when specific spots are called out by name in the job request form. See Section 5.

What we mean by cleaning

Standard for wipe-down surfaces (baseboards, door frames, trim, walls).

Across all surfaces in this category, "cleaning" means the following: dust removal (with particular attention to top edges where dust collects), followed by a wipe-down with a damp cloth and all-purpose cleaner to lift surface grime. Marks that come off with a normal single wipe pass — they come off. Anything beyond that is excluded from the standard scope.

Excluded from standard wipe-down:

  • Magic Eraser scrubbing of scuffs, scrapes, or marks
  • Heavy scuff, crayon, marker, or paint-mark removal
  • Stain removal requiring repeat passes or scrubbing
  • Any cleaning task that takes more than one normal wipe pass per area

These items can be requested as add-ons by calling out specific locations in the job request form (see Section 5 wall-cleaning policy and the Add-On Menu in Section 4) or are billable at the $50/hour targeted cleaning rate.

3. What’s Not Included

The following are excluded from all move-out cleanings unless specifically purchased as an add-on (Section 4) or called out in the job request (Section 7). This list is intentionally explicit to prevent scope disputes.

  • Exterior structures: exterior laundry rooms, sheds, garages, detached units — available as add-ons (see Section 4).
  • Outdoor areas: patios, balconies, walkways, exterior walls, exterior windows.
  • Wall stains, scuffs, or marks beyond standard wipe-down: not included unless specifically called out by location in the job request form. See Section 5 for wall-cleaning policy.
  • Ceiling cleaning or ceiling stains: excluded entirely. Stains on ceilings typically require paint touch-up, not cleaning. If you would like ceiling cleaning included, please call it out in the job request form so we can quote it.
  • Scuff or mark scrubbing on baseboards, door frames, or trim: not included unless called out. Standard scope is dust and wipe-down only.
  • Carpet shampooing, steam cleaning, or deep extraction
  • Wall hole repair, paint touch-up, drywall work, or any home repair
  • Personal item removal, decluttering, or trash beyond standard bagging
  • Metal vertical blinds (cannot be cleaned without damage)
  • Pet waste, biohazard, mold remediation
  • Furniture moving
  • Any cleaning task requiring more than one normal wipe pass

5. Condition Policy

Base estimates assume Standard condition. The job request form requires self-reporting of property condition using the following tiers:

  • Standard: normal wear, prior tenant cleaned before leaving, no heavy buildup.
  • Heavy: tenant did not clean, visible grime in kitchen/bath, soap scum, dust accumulation throughout.
  • Severe: neglected for extended period, grease layers, mold spots, heavy staining, requires significant time beyond estimate.

Time-overage rate. If the actual condition of the property exceeds Standard, the base estimate may be exceeded. Time beyond the estimated time range listed in the base pricing table bills at $100 per hour, billed in 30-minute increments. This applies regardless of the condition tier reported in the job request — if reported condition does not match actual condition on arrival, the overage rate still applies.

Wall spot-cleaning. Light stain or mark removal is included only when specific spots are called out by location in the job request form (for example: “stain on living room east wall near outlet,” “marks on bedroom 2 wall above light switch”). Generic requests such as “clean the walls” do not qualify and will bill at the $50/hour targeted wall-cleaning rate.

6. Callback Policy

Within 5 business days of job completion: if a missed or below-standard item from the original scope is reported in writing (email is acceptable), our team will return to correct it at no charge. The report must specify which room and which item.

After 5 business days, or for items outside the original scope: a touch-up fee applies, minimum $150 plus applicable add-on rates for any additional work requested.

New work requests: cleaning between tenants, jobs requested weeks or months after the original cleaning, and cleaning of tenant damage discovered later are not callbacks. These are quoted and invoiced as new jobs.

7. How to Request a Job

All cleaning requests must be submitted via the job request form: [Typeform link to be inserted]

Requests submitted via text, email, or phone outside the form will be returned with the form link before scheduling.

The form collects the following required information:

  1. Property address
  2. Approximate square footage
  3. Bedroom and bathroom count
  4. Requested service date (minimum 7 days out) and earliest acceptable start time
  5. Access instructions (lockbox code, key location, on-site contact)
  6. Property condition tier (Standard / Heavy / Severe — see Section 5)
  7. Any add-ons requested, with quantities where relevant (e.g., 12 window panes, 4 blind sets)
  8. Specific spots or areas that require attention — required if you want wall marks, problem areas, or anything beyond standard scope addressed under the included price
  9. Confirmation that property will be empty (no movers, no remaining belongings, no workers)
  10. Confirmation that running water and electricity are on
  11. Photos of problem areas (optional but recommended)

Lead time. Requests must be submitted at least 7 days in advance. Same-day or under-7-day requests are subject to availability and a rush fee, quoted at time of request.

A note on scope.

Our checklist defines what's included in every move-out cleaning, but every property is different. If a specific area needs extra attention — a heavy buildup spot, a stained surface, a high-traffic zone — call it out in the job request form. Without that flag, we'll clean to standard scope and can't be expected to identify property-specific priorities on our own.

8. Property Requirements

To complete a move-out cleaning to standard, the following conditions must be met at the start of the job. Failure to meet any of them results in a $79 same-day cancellation fee.

  • Property must be completely empty: no remaining belongings, no furniture, no trash.
  • No movers, contractors, workers, or other personnel may be on-site during the cleaning.
  • All cupboards, refrigerator, and storage spaces must be cleared.
  • Running water and electricity must be available throughout the property.
  • All access codes or keys must be functional and shared with us in the job request form.
